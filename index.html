Pedro Sánchez: Life and career in serving Spain

February 19, 2020February 19, 2020 Brian DukeUncategorized

Pedro Sánchez is a Spanish political figure who has served as Spain’s premier as of 2 June 2018. He grew up in a well-to-do Madrid family, the father’s son of a businessman and the mother of a civil servant.  He was lagging in the polls as Spain’s leader of the opposition in early 2018, battered by two resounding electoral losses. 

He studied economics at Madrid’s Complutense University, graduating in 1995, then finished two postgraduate courses, earning a Master’s degree in EU economics from the Free University of Brussels. He later lectured in economics in Spain and had a spell working for the High Representative of the United Nations in Bosnia and Herzegovina. He has two teenage daughters and is married.

But in May 2018 he took the opportunity to take power away from the conservative leader of the Popular Party (PP), Mariano Rajoy, who was struck by a huge political financing scandal. Mr. Rajoy was expelled in a vote of no confidence-a first for a post-Franco democratic era in Spain. Then last April Mr. Sánchez won his Spanish Socialist Workers ‘ Party (PSOE)’s first election victory since 2008.

He has ambitious plans to bring income tax rates for the highest-paid Spaniards and to undo labor market changes that date back to the previous conservative government, making it much easier to fire workers.

With Mr. Sánchez himself agreeing, it was difficult for him to embark on more ambitious, structural reforms, with his party short of a parliamentary majority. He suggests revising the education system, allowing euthanasia, reforming labor regulations, and shaking up national broadcaster RTVE.
